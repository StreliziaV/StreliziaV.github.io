What can
Computer Vision do ?

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence (AI) that enables computers and
systems to derive meaningful information from digital images, videos and other visual inputs
If AI enables computers to think, computer vision enables them to see, observe and understand.

Motivations
and
Histories

1959

Visual experiment
on cats

Two neurophysiologists showed a cat an array of images, attempting to correlate a response in its brain.

They discovered that it responded first to hard edges or lines, and scientifically, this meant that image processing starts with simple shapes like straight edges.

1965

Lawrence
Roberts

The publishment of Machine Perception of Three-Dimensional Solids pioneered computer vision research for the purpose of understanding three-dimensional scenes

After that, people began to conduct in-depth research on the world of building blocks, from edge detection, corner feature extraction, to line, plane, curve and other geometric element analysis, to image shading, texture, motion, and imaging geometry, etc., and established various Data structure and inference rules.

1970s

courses and clear
theoretical systems appeared

David Marr put forward the theory of computer vision (Computational Vision) in the AI laboratory of MIT, which is a completely different theory from the analysis method of the building block world led by Lawrence Roberts.

Computer vision theory became an important theoretical framework for computer vision in the 1980s, giving computer vision a clear system and promoting the development of computer vision.

1980s

theory moves from
laboratory to application

The publication of the book "Vision" (Marr, 1982) marked that computer vision has become an independent subject.

1982 DataMan, a vision system produced by Japan's COGEX company, is the world's first industrial optical character recognition (OCR) system.

2005

Image feature
engineering appeared

Dalal & Triggs proposed histogram of orientation gradients, and HOG (Histogram of Oriented Gradients) was applied to pedestrian detection.

It is a very commonly used feature method to describe the local texture of an image in the field of computer vision and pattern recognition.

2010 to now

Deep Learning
on Computer Vision

Research has gone from two-dimensional images to three-dimensional to video to real space detection.

The operation method has changed from constructing three-dimensional to feature recognition, and the algorithm has changed from shallow neural network to deep learning.
