What can
Computer Vision do ?

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence (AI) that enables computers and
systems to derive meaningful information from digital images, videos and other visual inputs
If AI enables computers to think, computer vision enables them to see, observe and understand.

Motivations
And
Histories

1959

Visual Experiment
on Cats

Two neurophysiologists showed a cat an array of images, attempting to correlate a response in its brain.

They discovered that it responded first to hard edges or lines, and scientifically, this meant that image processing starts with simple shapes like straight edges.

(click on the image for more information)

1965

Lawrence
Roberts

The publishment of Machine Perception of Three-Dimensional Solids pioneered computer vision research for the purpose of understanding three-dimensional scenes

After that, people began to conduct in-depth research on the world of building blocks, from edge detection, corner feature extraction, to line, plane, curve and other geometric element analysis, to image shading, texture, motion, and imaging geometry, etc., and established various Data structure and inference rules.

1970s

Courses and Clear
Theoretical Systems Appeared

David Marr put forward the theory of computer vision (Computational Vision) in the AI laboratory of MIT, which is a completely different theory from the analysis method of the building block world led by Lawrence Roberts.

Computer vision theory became an important theoretical framework for computer vision in the 1980s, giving computer vision a clear system and promoting the development of computer vision.

1980s

Theory Moves from
Laboratory to Application

The publication of the book "Vision" (Marr, 1982) marked that computer vision has become an independent subject.

1982 DataMan, a vision system produced by Japan's COGEX company, is the world's first industrial optical character recognition (OCR) system.

2005

Image Feature
Engineering Appeared

Dalal & Triggs proposed histogram of orientation gradients, and HOG (Histogram of Oriented Gradients) was applied to pedestrian detection.

It is a very commonly used feature method to describe the local texture of an image in the field of computer vision and pattern recognition.

2010 to now

Deep Learning
on Computer Vision

Research has gone from two-dimensional images to three-dimensional to video to real space detection.

The operation method has changed from constructing three-dimensional to feature recognition, and the algorithm has changed from shallow neural network to deep learning.

How
Computer Vision
Works

Computer vision needs lots of data which comes from image processing and feature extraction.

Then it runs analysis of those data over and over until it discerns distinctions and ultimately recognize images. For example, to train a computer to recognize bicycle, it needs to be fed vast quantities of bicycle images and other related items like motorcycle to learn the differences and recognize a bicycle.

These processes involves many math and statistical method. There are PCA and LDA for feature extraction processs. Also, deep learning and convolutional neural network (CNN) play an important role in image recognization and classification.

The following sections show a demo of the image recognization process.

Step 1

Digital Image
Acquisition

First, various photosensitive cameras, including remote sensing equipment, X-ray tomography, radar, etc, are needed to capture a digital image

The digital image can be represented in different format, such as RGB, YUV, or CMY for the convenience of the following operations. It can also be reprented by various physical data, such as the depth, absorbance or reflectance of sound waves, electromagnetic waves or nuclear magnetic resonance.

Step 2

Digital Image
Pre-Process

Before implementing the feature extraction, one or some preprocessing is often used to aid computational feasibility of subsequent processes.

These process may include Contrast Enhancement, Histogram Equalisation, Noise Reduction, or Edge Extraction. It depends on the specific demand of different computer vision system.

Click here for more information on image process->

Step 3

Feature
Extraction

After getting the row data, features such as the structural information, luminance, chrominance will be extracted by applying analysis methods.

Principle Component Analysis (PCA) can be used to reduce the dimension of the row data, and extract the most significant features among a digital image. In addition, Local Binary Patterns (LBP) and Linear Discrimination Analysis (LDA) are also efficient method to do the feature extraction. while PCA and LBP don't use any class information, LDA is a satisfactory method for classification.

Click here for more information on feature extraction->

Step 4

Classification
and
Recognization

This is the step to make decision and is the most complicated part among the whole process. Based on the feature vectors extracted before, the system will ultimately do the classification and recognization on the input data, and transform the result to the upper system.

Common methods to accomplish this task include Linear Decision Function within feature space, Minimum-Distance Classifiers, and Template Matching, etc.

However, there are several shortcomings of the common methods such as the time cost and accuracy. Nowadays, Deep Learning and Neural Networks methods are essential for this work, which enable the computer to recognize a image with neural system similar to human being.
